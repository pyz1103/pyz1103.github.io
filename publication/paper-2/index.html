Game Analysis of Traffic Conflict between Pedestrian and Vehicles on Unsignalized Road Section Based on Cumulative Prospect Theory

Lunhui Xu, Yuzhuang Pian, Yongjie Lin, Zilin Huang
September 2021
PDF Cite
Image credit: Vehicle-person conflict situation

Abstract

Unsignalized road sections have strong interaction and high accident rate. Due to the lack of traffic signals to separate people and vehicles, it is easy to form conflicts between people and vehicles, which affects road traffic efficiency and traffic safety. Therefore, this article aims at minimizing the loss of each road user, and a Cumulative-prospect-based Multiparty Conflict Game was established. By studying the interaction between pedestrians and vehicles in the process of travel, this paper analyzed the influencing factors of the decision-making behavior of both sides and constructed the gain and loss matrix. Taking into account the subjective psychological perception of decision-makers, the dating cumulative prospect theory and the conformity effect optimized the decision probability and profited function of the model. In this article, the replicator dynamics were used to solve the model, and the evolution trend of each system under different traffic conditions was analyzed by numerical simulation to verify the effectiveness of the method. The results show that the stable state and evolution direction of the system are mainly affected by the risk cost, conformity effect weight and percentage of pedestrians crossing the street, which can form four different traffic scenarios":" “people give right of way to cars”, “cars give right of way to people”, “people and cars give right of way to each other” and “people and cars collide”. In addition, the CTR data set is used for parameter calibration and model calibration of the model, and the convergence rate of the model is analyzed by the index of the number of system iterations. Compared with the EMCG model, the CPMCG model has a significant improvement in the system convergence speed, the average convergence speed is increased by 45.83%, and the model decision accuracy is increased by 9.44% and 7.49% for pedestrians and vehicles, respectively. In addition, the relationship between the evolution trend of the system and the key parameters such as risk cost, concession loss and conformity effect was also studied. The results show that when the conformity effect weight is low, the increase of the concession loss will promote the increase and restrain the decrease of the respective “pass” decision-making probability. As the weight increases to the threshold , the increase in the concession loss will inhibit the rise and promote the decline. And the increase in the cost of risk is necessary to demonstrate the realistic effect. At the same time, the increase in the proportion of pedestrians crossing the street will promote the increase in the probability of pedestrian passage and restrain its downward trend, and the increase in weight will intensify its degree of change. The theoretical model established in this paper can provide a reasonable theoretical basis for the traffic management department to formulate policies and regulations in the accident-prone road sections.
